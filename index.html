Common Upgrade Questions

Things Homeowners Ask Before Investing in Efficient Systems

Practical answers to help you decide which upgrades make sense for your Florida home.

How much can I really save by switching to a tankless water heater?

Most Fort Myers homeowners see 25–35% lower water heating costs after upgrading to a tankless unit. Florida's mild climate makes tankless even more efficient, since incoming water temperatures stay higher year-round. Plus, units typically last 20+ years vs. 10–12 for traditional tanks.

Do I really need a water softener in Fort Myers?

If you've noticed scale on faucets, dry skin after showers, or appliances failing earlier than expected — yes. Lee County water is moderately to very hard depending on your area. A water softener protects pipes, water heaters, dishwashers, and washing machines from premature scale damage.

What's the lifespan of plumbing in a typical Fort Myers home?

Copper lasts 50+ years, PEX 40+, but galvanized steel often fails at 40–50. Many homes built in the 1970s and earlier in Edison Park or McGregor are now due for replacement. Our camera inspections can show you exactly what shape your pipes are in.

Are rebates available for high-efficiency installations?

Yes — many manufacturers offer rebates on tankless water heaters, high-SEER HVAC systems, and ENERGY STAR appliances. We help you identify and apply for available rebates as part of your installation quote.

How long does a typical efficiency upgrade take?

Most water heater swaps take 4–6 hours. A new HVAC install is typically a one-day job. Bathroom remodels and full repipes take longer — usually 3–5 days — but we coordinate the schedule to minimize disruption.

Will efficient upgrades add value to my home?

Definitely. Modern HVAC systems, tankless water heaters, water filtration, and updated plumbing are top selling points for Florida buyers — especially in vacation and seasonal home markets where buyers want move-in-ready properties.
